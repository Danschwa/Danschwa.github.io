RISE

"...space is for everybody. It's not just for a few people in science or math, or for a select group of astronauts.
That's our new frontier out there, and it's everybody's business to know about space." - Christa McAuliffe

Through our education programs, our group trains new members with little to no prior experience and therefore ensures that future innovation and achievement are possible. During the Fall of 2017, the Rocket Project at UCLA ran its first iteration of the Rocketry Introduction for Student Education (RISE) training program with great success. In collaboration with the MAE department, a formal UCLA seminar course will be beginning in the Fall of 2018 that will be based off of this program and taught by the undergraduates that led it.

Beginning on October 9th, 2017 and concluding on December 8th, 2017, RISE catered to roughly 100 UCLA students interested in rocketry. The overwhelming majority of these individuals were students with majors inside the Henry Samueli School of Engineering and Applied Sciences, and roughly 65% of participants were in their first quarter at UCLA. Over the course of the program, one weekly seminar was held at 5pm each Monday in EIV-138 and these seminars were complimented by workshop times throughout the week. Within the first two weeks of the program, students were grouped into teams of 2-4 students and given the project of building their own apogee maximizing rocket to be powered by a commercial e-motor of total impulse close to 25Ns. Two of the seminars featured experienced members of URP who lectured on relevant topics such as drag and flight dynamics. Guest lectures were also given by Dr. Pineda and Dr. Spearrin who spoke about Combustion Chemistry and Manufacturing Techniques, respectively. Besides these guest lectures, programming and instruction was mainly completed by RISE Director Caleb Lessard-Clouston with assistance from assistant coordinator Anthony Smolyanov and URP External Vice President Christopher Vincent.

Upon the conclusion of the program, it is estimated that 45-60 of the original 100 participants are involved with URP. Of these new members, roughly 60% are active members of Project Prometheus and many have taken on leadership roles.
