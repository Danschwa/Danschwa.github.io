ARES

Rocket Project at UCLA transitioned many members to a liquid bipropellant program in late 2016, culminating in a team known as Project Ares. Ares builds our most complex, ambitious vehicles- pushing the envelope for what a student organization can do in the rocketry field. Our guiding principles are that mission success must drive all decisions, frequent testing is critical, and overcomplication leads to failure.

In the 2017-2018 year, this resulted in UCLA taking the apogee record for a student-built liquid engine at 12,500 feet. Now Ares is aiming to shatter the record again. With a predicted apogee of over 45,000 feet, the next rocket in the series will demonstrate important structural improvements and more advanced propulsion technologies. This gives Rocket Project at UCLA the opportunity to win multiple competitions, secure future funding, and inspire future generations of Bruins.

Technical Details
Performance
Predicted Apogee: 46,000 feet (2 lbm payload); 40,000 feet (10 lbm payload)

Propulsion
Propellants: LOx Ethanol, LOx Kerosene
Thrust: 700 lbf

Airframe
Length: 15.2 ft
Diameter: 7 in
Structure: Semi-Monocoque
Recovery: Dual Deployment, Two Bay
